# yes

Here’s the shortlist I’d use for a remixable Google shopping assistant project:

## Best starting points

- **Shopping assistant prebuilt agent**: the closest match for a personal shopping or product-discovery assistant; it supports product queries, recommendations, and order-style workflows, and is meant to be imported and configured.[^3_2]
- **AI Studio Agents templates**: useful if you want a lighter prototype path inside Google’s AI tooling, since the Agents area includes prebuilt templates you can adapt.[^3_12]
- **Google’s broader agentic commerce tooling**: useful if your goal is a more advanced shopping flow that spans discovery, comparison, and checkout-style actions.[^3_5][^3_6]


## Which one fits your laptop project

- For a **computer purchase assistant**, the shopping assistant prebuilt agent is the best fit because it already matches the “browse, compare, recommend” pattern.[^3_2]
- For a quick personal demo, AI Studio templates are probably easier to remix first.[^3_12]
- For a more ambitious build, Google’s retail and commerce agent stack points toward end-to-end shopping flows with richer integrations.[^3_6][^3_5]


## What I’d pick

If your goal is to build a **laptop recommender** for your own buying project, start with the shopping assistant prebuilt agent, then replace retail catalog logic with your spec filters:

- 64 GB RAM.
- RTX 4070 or 4080.
- 2 TB NVMe SSD.
- 16+ core CPU.
- strong cooling and upgradeability.[^3_2]

That gives you the fastest path from template to something useful for your needs.[^3_12][^3_2]
